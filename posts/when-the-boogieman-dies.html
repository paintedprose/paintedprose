When the Boogieman Dies

Nov. 10, 2025 · Poetry

Rejoice.
 Rejoice that fear no longer clings,
 that I need not shrink beneath its wings.

Embrace the sunlight again,
 remember what it was before,
 the pain, the fear, the grotesque sway
 that shadowed every passing day.

At one point it was a child,
 a lover, quiet, meek, and mild,
 a friend, a spouse, a parent too,
 the boogieman now made human through.

No longer permanent, yet weight remains.
 Who carries the memory of its chains?
Only the things it left behind
 can bear the echoes trapped in mind.

I thought the earth could hold its end,
 but I became the hope it failed to send.
A small flame flickered, almost gone,
 I carry both its grief and song.

I am its daughter,
 its victim,
 its remnants,
 drawn back always to that ghost.

A spirit it forgot to honor,
 a joy it once wanted to flower.

But what do I do with the pain?
 It has not left; the scars still ache during the rain.

Even now, the quiet hums with sorrow.
 Who can I fault for what was borrowed?
It was shaped by hands before my time,
 by echoes carried down the line.

Origins no longer matter.
 Yet still they whisper, faint and scattered.
And from that grief, I bear the flame—
 a quiet witness to its name.
