Drought

Mar 20, 2025 · Poetry

I keep trying to pour, pour, and pour. Now this well is running dry. The glass is no longer half full; it’s two sips from empty.

I’m parched yet no longer thirsty. I wish that I didn’t have more to fill. More to bury. More to mourn.

I wish the clock would break, so I no longer had to hear time tick past me. There are not enough words, wards, or worlds to escape to.

There is only an empty well. A pit. A vacuum. A black hole. Where it takes all the light and the stars once it loses its brilliance.

And sometimes I hope it takes me too.
